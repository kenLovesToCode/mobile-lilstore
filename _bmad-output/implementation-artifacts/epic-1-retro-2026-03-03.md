## 3) What Went Well

- Strong security posture was established early and improved iteratively (no plaintext credential storage, stronger KDF defaults, safer auth error behavior).
- Entry-gate and admin-session behavior became reliable through repeated hardening (snapshot lifecycle controls, retry/error handling, route guard improvements).
- Test strategy matured from helper-only checks to mounted integration and process-level verification, reducing behavior gaps.
- Story-to-story continuity was strong: each story reused and improved prior architecture instead of introducing disconnected patterns.
- PRD/architecture alignment stayed high, especially around offline-first behavior and strict role-gated flows.

## 4) Challenges and Growth Areas

- Epic 1 had repeated review loops caused by quality gaps that were not caught early enough (race conditions, timing side-channel concerns, integration coverage gaps).
- Acceptance-evidence alignment needed multiple corrections (especially around AC4 performance evidence wording and checklist consistency).
- Several security hardening requirements emerged late in Story 1.3 and required deeper refactors than expected.
- Confidence in startup-performance acceptance remains constrained by environment/tooling limits for objective benchmark capture.

## 5) Story-Level Pattern Synthesis

### Common Struggles

- Recurring need to convert unit-style confidence into screen-level integration confidence.
- Repeated adjustments to startup/auth flow resilience under edge conditions (timeouts, stale cache/snapshot, retries).
- Quality documentation and implementation status drift required explicit cleanup in story artifacts.

### Recurring Review Feedback

- "Prove behavior end-to-end, not only helper outputs."
- "Preserve non-enumerating auth behavior and close timing/security gaps."
- "Keep state transitions deterministic under rapid user interaction."
- "Ensure story documentation matches actual acceptance evidence."

### Breakthrough Moments

- Introduction of mounted Expo Router integration tests for gate/auth flows.
- Formal hardening of auth KDF policy and non-downgrade behavior.
- Session handling and logout behavior became robust and regression-tested.
